  • A computational simulation of a collection of simple “atoms” which are free to move in two dimensions. The force between pairs of atoms is modeled using a Lennard-Jones potential.

    So that you can more easily explore how temperature and intermolecular attraction strength affect phase changes, intermolecular attractions are much stronger in this simulation than they are for the equivalent “featureless” and nonreactive atoms found in nature, those of the noble gases.

    When the Coulomb force interaction checkbox is checked, atoms also experience electrostatic interactions. Opposite charges of +1 or -1 are evenly distributed among the atoms, and then particles with opposite charge attract each other according to Coulomb's Law, while particles with the same charge repel. Particles with a positive charge appear blue and those with a negative charge appear red.
