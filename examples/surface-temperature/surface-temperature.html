Earth's Surface Temperature: Years 500-2009

Can you change the scales to make it appear that global temperature is not increasing?

About the Graph

  • Drag on the canvas to translate/pan the graph.
  • double-click on the canvas to zoom in
  • shift-double-click on the canvas to zoom out
  • Drag on one of the X or Y axis numeric labels to re-scale that axis
  • click on a data point to select it
  • hold the ALT/Option key down and click an empty area of the graph to add a data point

About the Data

The data are derived from Mann's reconstruction of Earth's surface temperature from 500 to 2009

References:

  • SST [K] Global SST reconstruction data
  • Global Signatures and Dynamical Origins of the Little Ice Age and Medieval Climate Anomaly (NOAA Paleoclimatology site)
  • Global Signatures and Dynamical Origins of the Little Ice Age and Medieval Climate Anomaly (pdf of original paper)
  • Time series annual Mann global SST
  • Jones et al., 1999, Surface Air Temperature and its Changes over the Past 150 Years

There's still a bug where if you scale an axis once, you can then translate/pan around but if you scale the same axis a second time when you next try and translate/pan the result of the first axis re-scale will re-appear. This bug also appears if you re-scale the axes and then add a new data point.
